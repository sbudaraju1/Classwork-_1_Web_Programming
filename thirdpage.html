Brownies

    Here is the recipe for the best brownies!

    1. Prep your tin with butter and parchment paper.
    2. Chop 8 oz of any chocolate bar.
    3. Mix 1/4 cup cocoa powder, 1 tbsp espresso powder, and 1 & 1/4 cups of hot, melted, unsalted butter in a bowl.
      • You can use dutch processed cocoa powder for the best flavor, but normal cocoa powder is also fine.
      • Once you pour the butter, let it sit for 2 minutes before mixing.
    4. Take a bowl and add 2 cups granulated sugar, 1/2 cup dark brown sugar, 2 tsp vanilla extract, and 2 tsp kosher salt.
    5. In another bowl, crack 6 eggs. Then slowly mix them into your sugar mixture.
      • Make sure to incorporate a TON of air into your egg-sugar mixture. Mixing it for atleast 10 minutes makes a big difference on how it rises.
      • Do not use baking powder as a substitute. This leads to a cakey brownie.
    6. Add the chocolate ganache made earlier into the egg-sugar mixture. Continue to fluff it up with air.
    7. Sift 1 cup all-purpose flour and 1/2 cup cocoa powder into the mixture.
      • Using a spatula, fold the dry ingredients into the mixture without deflating the air that was added in.
    8. Pour the batter into the prepped tin and smooth it out to ensure it is level.
      • Feel free to add any extra toppings that you love!
    9. Bake for 20 minutes at 350 F (180 C).
    10. Pull it out of the oven and smack it on the counter to crack the top surface and allow for evenness.
    11. Sprinkle some sea salt on top and place it back into the oven for another 25 minutes at 350 F (180 C).
    12. Let cool for 15 minutes before you cut and serve!
